Business Insights
Insight 1: Overall Customer Churn
Observation

The bank has 10,000 customers, of which 2,037 customers have exited, resulting in an overall churn rate of approximately 20.37%.

Business Impact

Nearly one in every five customers has left the bank, indicating a significant loss of potential revenue and customer lifetime value.

Recommendation

Implement proactive customer retention programs by identifying at-risk customers early and offering personalized engagement strategies.

Insight 2: Country-wise Churn Analysis
Observation

Germany records the highest customer churn rate (32.44%), compared to Spain (16.67%) and France (16.15%).

Business Impact

Customers in Germany are leaving the bank at nearly twice the rate of customers in France and Spain, suggesting regional differences in customer experience or service effectiveness.

Recommendation

Conduct a detailed investigation into customer behavior in Germany and introduce targeted retention campaigns to address region-specific challenges.

Insight 3: Gender-wise Churn Analysis
Observation

Female customers have a higher churn rate than male customers across all three countries.

Business Impact

This consistent trend indicates that current banking products or services may not be meeting the expectations of female customers.

Recommendation

Analyze customer feedback and product usage among female customers to design more personalized banking services and loyalty programs.

Insight 4: Age Group Analysis
Observation

Senior Adult and Senior Citizen customer groups exhibit the highest churn rates across multiple countries, while younger customers show comparatively lower churn.

Business Impact

Older customers represent a higher retention risk despite often having longer banking relationships and potentially higher account balances.

Recommendation

Develop specialized retention initiatives for older customers, such as relationship management services, personalized financial advice, and exclusive benefits.

Insight 5: Active Member Analysis
Observation

Inactive members consistently show higher churn rates than active members across all countries.

Business Impact

Customer engagement is strongly associated with retention. Less active customers are significantly more likely to leave the bank.

Recommendation

Increase customer engagement through personalized offers, digital banking campaigns, and reward programs that encourage regular account activity.

Insight 6: Product Analysis
Observation

Customers with specific product counts (particularly two or more products, depending on the dashboard) demonstrate noticeable differences in churn behaviour.

Business Impact

The number of products owned by a customer influences loyalty and retention, making product ownership an important business indicator.

Recommendation

Identify customers with lower product adoption and promote cross-selling opportunities that increase customer engagement and long-term retention.
